Members of Writ Large have access to the Critique Partner Database at the Ottawa Writer's Circle. With an account on the OWC website you also get access to their forums, blog, and other resources.

Writing Craft

  1. King, Stephen. On Writing: 10th Anniversary Edition. URL KFPL

    Equal parts biography and advice, with plenty of practical examples. Find out what Stephen King believes should be in every writer's toolbox.

  2. Bird, Matt. The Secrets of Story: Innovative Tools for Perfecting Your Fiction and Captivating Readers URL

    The pitch for this book is a parable: "You’ve just boarded a plane. You’ve loaded your phone with your favorite podcasts, but before you can pop in your earbuds, disaster strikes: The guy in the next seat starts telling you all about something crazy that happened to him–in great detail. This is the unwelcome storyteller, trying to convince a reluctant audience to care about his story. We all hate that guy, right? But when you tell a story (any kind of story: a novel, a memoir, a screenplay, a stage play, a comic, or even a cover letter), you become the unwelcome storyteller. So how can you write a story that audiences will embrace? The answer is simple: Remember what it feels like to be that jaded audience. Tell the story that would win you over, even if you didn’t want to hear it."

  3. Callahan, Diane. Quotidian Writer YouTube Channel. URL

    Ten to thirty minute videos covering topics such as how to write a first line, how to build tension in a scene, and how to show and not tell.

  4. Weiland, K.M. Helping Writers Become Authors. URL

    Weekly blog posts on a wide variety of writing topics.

  5. Marais, Bianca. The Shit No One Tells You About Writing. URL

    How do you balance heartbreak and humour? How do you maintain suspense? How do you write a sex scene? Three seasons and counting of an interview-style podcast covering a broad range or writing topics.

Story Structure

  1. Weiland, K.M. Helping Writers Become Authors. URL

    One of this website's great features is an in-depth look into story structure using the three-act format. Narrative points in any plot are discussed theoretically and examples are given from popular literature and movies.

World Building

  1. Reynolds, Alistair, Nnedi Okorafor, Ann Leckie, Becky Chambers, Kim Stanley Robinson and M John Harrison. 'If the aliens lay eggs, how does that affect architecture?': sci-fi writers on how they build their worlds. URL

    A collection of science fiction writers on the topic of how exactly they build their worlds.

Critique Circles

  1. Inked Voices Critique Cheat Sheet. URL

    This is the guide to critiquing that we use for our critique circles. Not only does it contain points to consider as a critiquer, these also double as points to keep in mind as a writer too!

Publication

  1. Maum, Courtney. Before and After the Book Deal: A Writer's Guide to Finishing, Publishing, Promoting, and Surviving Your First Book. URL KFPL

    All the way from the beginning of your idea until the last penny trickles in, what do writers, agents, and editors think you should know?
